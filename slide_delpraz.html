Indications

  • Healing of erosive esophagitis (up to 8 weeks)
  • Maintenance of healing & relief of heartburn (up to 6 months)
  • Treatment of symptomatic non-erosive GERD (4 weeks)
* Use only as directed by a registered physician

Pharmacology

Dexlansoprazole is a proton pump inhibitor (PPI) that suppresses gastric acid secretion by inhibiting the (H+/K+)-ATPase enzyme in gastric parietal cells. It is the R-enantiomer of lansoprazole. The dual delayed release (DDR) formulation creates two distinct plasma peaks after dosing.

Dosage

  • Erosive esophagitis: 60 mg once daily (up to 8 weeks)
  • Symptomatic GERD: 30 mg once daily (4 weeks)
  • Maintenance & heartburn relief: 30 mg once daily (up to 6 months)

Administration

Can be taken with or without food. Capsules can be opened and granules sprinkled on a tablespoon and swallowed immediately. Do not chew granules.

If a dose is missed, take as soon as possible. Do not double dose.

Interactions

Drug interactions: Atazanavir, Warfarin, Tacrolimus, Clopidogrel, Methotrexate.

Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to any component of the formulation.

Side Effects

Common: Diarrhea, abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, flatulence.

Pregnancy & Lactation

Category B. No adequate studies in pregnant or breastfeeding women.

Precautions & Warnings

Includes risks of gastric malignancy, Clostridium difficile infection, bone fracture, hypomagnesemia, and interaction with Methotrexate.

Use in Special Populations

  • Children under 12: Safety not established
  • Elderly: No dose adjustment needed
  • Renal impairment: No adjustment needed
  • Moderate hepatic impairment: Max 30 mg/day

Overdose

No serious effects reported even at 120–300 mg doses.

Therapeutic Class

Proton Pump Inhibitor (PPI)

Storage

Store below 30°C, in a dry place, away from light. Keep out of reach of children.
