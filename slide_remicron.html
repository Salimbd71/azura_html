Indications

Used for type 2 diabetes when diet, exercise and weight loss do not suffice.

Pharmacology

Stimulates insulin release from pancreatic beta cells and reduces hepatic glucose output.

Dosage & Administration

Recommended dose: 1 to 4 tablets (30-120 mg) once daily with breakfast. Do not chew or crush. Always take after eating.

  • If you forget a dose, take the next dose at the usual time.
  • Do not take a double dose.
  • In overdose, seek immediate medical help — symptoms include low blood sugar.

Interactions

May interact with insulin, antibiotics, antifungals, beta-blockers, corticosteroids, etc. Avoid alcohol. Monitor blood sugar closely.

Contraindications

  • Type 1 diabetes
  • Severe kidney or liver disease
  • During breastfeeding
  • Known allergy to sulfonylureas

Side Effects

Common: Hypoglycemia. Others: liver issues, rash, digestive discomfort, visual disturbance.

Pregnancy & Lactation

Not recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

Precautions & Warnings

Monitor blood glucose regularly. Risk of hypoglycemia is high during early treatment and fasting. Always carry sugar with you.

Storage

Store below 30°C, protect from light and moisture. Keep out of children’s reach.

General Instructions

  • Take before breakfast daily
  • Do not miss meals
  • Carry sugary snack to manage hypoglycemia
  • Follow doctor’s instructions strictly
