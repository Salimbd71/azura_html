Indications

  • Primary hyperlipidemia and mixed dyslipidemia (adjunct to diet)
  • Hypertriglyceridemia (adjunct to diet)
  • Primary dysbetalipoproteinemia (Type III)
* Use only as directed by a registered physician

Pharmacology

Rosuvastatin is a selective and competitive inhibitor of HMG-CoA reductase. It increases hepatic LDL receptors and inhibits hepatic synthesis of VLDL, reducing both VLDL and LDL particles.

Dosage & Administration

  • Pediatric (10–17 years): 5–20 mg/day (max 20 mg)
  • Very high cholesterol or risk of MI/stroke: 40 mg/day
  • Homozygous familial hypercholesterolemia: Start 20 mg/day
  • Asian patients: Start 5 mg/day
  • With Cyclosporine: Limit to 5 mg/day
  • With Lopinavir/Ritonavir or Atazanavir/Ritonavir: Limit to 10 mg/day
  • With Gemfibrozil: Avoid if possible; if needed, limit to 10 mg/day
  • Severe renal impairment: Start 5 mg/day, max 10 mg/day

Interactions

  • Cyclosporine: Limit dose to 5 mg
  • Gemfibrozil: Avoid; if used, max 10 mg
  • Lopinavir/Ritonavir: Limit to 10 mg
  • Anticoagulants: Monitor INR
  • Niacin/Fibrates: May increase risk of muscle effects

Contraindications

  • Hypersensitivity to components
  • Liver disease or elevated liver enzymes
  • Pregnant or breastfeeding women

Side Effects

Common: Headache, myalgia, constipation, weakness, abdominal pain, nausea.

Pregnancy & Lactation

Not recommended. Discontinue if pregnant. Unknown if excreted in breast milk.

Precautions & Warnings

Caution in patients with risk factors for myopathy. Discontinue if muscle pain or CK elevation occurs. Monitor liver enzymes at baseline, 12 weeks, and periodically.

Overdose

No specific treatment. Supportive care as needed. Hemodialysis not effective.

Therapeutic Class

Statins, Other anti-anginal & anti-ischemic drugs

Storage

Store below 30°C, protected from light and moisture. Keep out of children's reach.

Quick Tips

  • Take with diet and exercise for best results
  • May cause stomach upset — take with food
  • Report any muscle pain or fatigue
  • Check liver function before and during use
  • Avoid in pregnancy and breastfeeding
